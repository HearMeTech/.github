# HearMeTech Communication Policy

At HearMeTech, our mission requires deep, focused work. This policy isn't for bureaucracy; it's to protect your time, focus, and to build a transparent, effective team.

Our key principle is: **"Asynchronous by default, synchronous when necessary."**

We respect each other's time and understand that the best work (especially in R&D and engineering) requires long periods of uninterrupted focus.

## 2. Key Communication Principles

1.  **Async is Respect.**
    * When you send a message, don't expect an instant reply.
    * Formulate your message completely so the recipient doesn't have to ask clarifying questions. (Bad: "Hey, you there?" / Good: "Hey, when you have a moment, please look at PR #123. I'm unsure about the error handling in `auth.py`.")

2.  **Transparency by Default.**
    * Default to public channels in Slack and discussions in GitHub Issues. This allows the whole team to learn and stay in context.

3.  **You Are Not Your Calendar.**
    * Block "Focus Time" in your calendar for deep work.
    * Turn off Slack notifications when you are in focus. This is not just allowed; it's encouraged.

4.  **Empathy and Respect.**
    * Remember there is a human behind every avatar.
    * All our communications must adhere to our **[Code of Conduct (`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`)](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)**.
